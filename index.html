Bernoulli's Principle & Applications

Physics • 12 Min Read

In fluid dynamics, Bernoulli's principle states that an increase in the speed of a fluid occurs simultaneously with a decrease in static pressure or a decrease in the fluid's potential energy.

Fig 1.1: Venturi Meter Diagram

Equation: P + 0.5ρv² + ρgh = constant

This principle is critical in understanding aerodynamic lift. When air flows over an airfoil, the pressure differential creates an upward force.
