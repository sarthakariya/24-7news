Computational Analysis of Boundary Layer Separation in High-Reynolds-Number Flows using Lattice Boltzmann Methods

A. J. Whitman, R. K. Singh, and E. Schrodinger

Department of Physics, University of Oxford, OX1 3PU, United Kingdom

(Received 12 November 2025; published 3 February 2026)

ABSTRACT

We present a numerical study of turbulent boundary layer separation over a curved surface using the Lattice Boltzmann Method (LBM). Unlike traditional Navier-Stokes solvers, LBM provides a mesoscopic description of fluid dynamics. The simulation results indicate that at $Re > 10^6$, the adverse pressure gradient induces a separation bubble.

I. INTRODUCTION

The control of flow separation is of paramount importance in aerodynamic design. Prandtl's boundary layer theory simplifies the momentum equation to:

$$ u \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} + v \frac{\partial u}{\partial y} = -\frac{1}{\rho}\frac{dP}{dx} + \nu \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial y^2} $$

When $\frac{dP}{dx} > 0$ (adverse pressure gradient), the fluid particles decelerate, leading to separation [2].
